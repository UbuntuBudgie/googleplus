Good day, Ubuntu-Budgie fans just a quick note. For those using UB on a laptop, as I do, and are scratching their heads as regards to why the absolute f*@k you can't right-click on the touchpad?...Yeah, me too!
Fortunately, I'd come across this on Antergos, a while back, so knew exactly what it was. When moving from 3.26 to 3.28, the absolute Geniuses at the Gnome decided that the default method to use either 1, 2 or 3 fingers to left, right and middle click.
For the rest of us that, I don't know, think that maybe left click on the pad should mean left, right should be say right and bottom middle click for middle, here's the solution:

sudo apt install gnome-tweaks

Then simply open Gnome-Tweaks/Keyboard & Mouse and change the touchpad setting from "Fingers" to "Area". There's also a switch for "Middle click to paste" which is quite useful.

Or, of course, you can try and re-map your muscle memory to use the non-intuitive default method..Yeah me neither!

Not really ideal to have to install that, but there we go...
